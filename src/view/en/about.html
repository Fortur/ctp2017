About the Research

General Information

In 2017, the Russian Regional Integrated Reporting Network (RRN) conducted the Vth annual national survey on corporate transparency of the largest Russian companies.

Research Partners:

  • “Da-Strategy” Group of Companies;
  • Engineering division of state Corporation “Rosatom” ASE;
  • “Nexia Pacioli” Group of Companies;
  • Institute for Economic Strategies of the Russian Academy of Sciences;
  • Downstream Technologies;
  • International Investment Legal Agency.

Transparency and information disclosure of companies are important elements of their business attractiveness and allow the key stakeholders to make accurate decisions, including investment ones. Transparency is one of the factors contributing to business value growth. It is characterized by the company’s eagerness to present management information to the stakeholders. Corporate transparency is equally important for public acceptance of company’s activity.

The main goal of the research is to conduct an annual assessment of the level of corporate transparency through a comprehensive analysis of public reports and corporate sites of the largest Russian companies, including in order to create conditions for a qualitative evolution of corporate transparency.

Tasks:

  • survey of corporate transparency of the largest Russian companies;
  • identification of the best corporate transparency practices, including information disclosure via reporting;
  • preparation of ranking and sub-ratings of corporate transparency.

Subject of the Research:

The research surveys public annual reports of companies for 2016 (integrated reports, standard annual reports and non-financial reports - environmental reports, social reports, sustainability reports, corporate social responsibility reports, etc.). In particular, the information on the use of the following disclosure standards in the reports is checked:

  • Regulation on the disclosure of information by the issuers of equity securities (approved by the Bank of Russia on December 30, 2014 No. 454-P);
  • The International Framework;
  • Global Reporting Initiative (different versions).

In addition, information, posted on official corporate websites of companies, is examined based on a certain set of criteria. 1 .

Other reporting information (quarterly reports, financial statements, etc.), as well as a news line, presentations and individual internal documents posted on official corporate sites of companies, are considered only when they are specifically referred to by annual reports as a source of additional reporting information.

Reporting information for 2017 is not surveyed.

Information in foreign languages is not examined.

In 2017, there were a number of changes in the methodology and organization of the Study.

Sample:

Within the framework of the Study, the following companies are surveyed:

  • included in the rating "Rating of 600 top companies in Russia in 2015" ("RAEX-600");
  • listed on the Moscow and foreign exchanges (LSE (London Stock Exchange), NYSE (New York Stock Exchange), NASDAQ (National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotation), HKEX (Hong Kong Exchange), Euronext NV, Deutsche Börse Group);
  • companies that sent an official application letter requesting to be included in the study sample before November 1, 2017. The decision to include a specific company in the sample is taken by the Expert Council;
  • Companies included in the list of backbone organizations (minutes of the meeting of the Government Commission for Economic Development and Integration No. 1 of 05.02.2015), as well as state corporations and companies managed by them with revenues of at least three billion rubles (according to the data for 2015).

Company survey:

Starting 2017, companies are distributed by transparency level according to the number of points scored. The methodological calculation of scores takes into account the importance of the degree of compliance of the information disclosed to the requirements of Russian and international reporting standards, the availability / absence of verification of reporting information by an independent party / parties and the availability of an electronic (interactive) annual report as one of the mechanisms ensuring the convenience of using disclosed information for the stakeholders of companies.
