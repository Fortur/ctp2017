Description of Corporate Governance System

Disclosure of Information on the Corporate Governance System

Mainly due to the legislative regulations the information on the corporate governance system is the most referable to disclose by the leaders of the group (Premium, I and II transparency levels). The transparency reports of all 48 companies disclose the corporate governance system, namely, the structure and interaction of management bodies. Disclosure of such information is mandatory if the company shares are admitted for trading in accordance with the Regulation on the Disclosure of Information by the Issuers of Securities of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation 454-P as of 30.12.2015 (paragraph 70.4) 18.

More than 90% of the companies disclosed the information about the main documents regulating the corporate governance system (45 out of 48 companies), as well as the availability and composition of committees under the Board of Directors or the fact of their absence (45 out of 48 companies), the use of Russian (Code of Corporate Governance) and / or international (as a rule, these are the requirements of the London Stock Exchange) corporate management standards (43 of 48 companies).

Leading companies which disclosed the Corporate Governance System (2016-2017)

The activities planned to improve the corporate governance system are disclosed a little worse. This information was provided by 73% of the leading group companies (35 out of 48 companies). About 40 out of 48 (83%) companies disclosed the information on the improvements of the corporate governance system during the reporting period.

Disclosure of remuneration

The most of the leading group companies (Premium, I and II levels of transparency) disclosed the information on the remuneration policy in 2017. Such are the main approaches / principles (47 out of 48 companies). This is understandable, since the disclosure of this information is mandatory in accordance with the disclosure regulations applied to the issuers of securities by the Central Bank of the Russian Federation 454-P as of 30.12.2015 (paragraph 70.3) 19.

39 companies disclosed the information on the structure and the amount of the remuneration of the members of the Board of Directors (81.25% of the leading group). More disclosed is the information on the structure of the remuneration of the CEO / President / Chairman of the Board (42 out of 48 companies). The Central Bank of the Russian Federation recommended issuers to disclose the remuneration of all the top managers in December 2017. Due to this fact this index may improve in the next fiscal year.

Compliance with the principles of the Corporate Governance Code

In 2014 the Central Bank of the Russian Federation adopted the Code of Corporate Governance. The Code is a set of recommendations providing for a better corporate management, instructing on the protection of the rights of shareholders, promoting their implementation in practice, ensuring a better company management, long-term and sustainable development.

The application of the provisions of the Code by the companies is voluntary and is motivated by the desire to increase the attractiveness of a company for actual and potential investors.

Corporate governance should be based on the principle of the sustainable development of the society and increase the efficiency of investments in the long term.

36 out of the 48 companies of the leading group have provided a table showing the information on compliance / non-compliance with the principles and recommendations of the Code of Corporate Governance, approved by the Central Bank of the Russian Federation.
