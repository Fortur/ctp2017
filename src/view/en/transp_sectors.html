Corporate Transparency of Various Sectors of the Russian Economy

Transparency coefficient

To evaluate and compare transparency of companies, united in different groups based on a significant characteristic, the Transparency Coefficient was introduced. The formula for Transparency Coefficient calculation is

The Coefficient allows comparing, for example, corporate transparency of one sector of economy with corporate transparency in another sector, transparency of companies listed on stock exchanges with transparency of non-listed companies and so forth.

Transparency of the various sectors of the Russian Federation (by the re-examined companies)

For reference: data are presented on the basis of the sample of 2017 (956 companies), the coefficient is calculated using the current methodology.

Conclusions:

  1. The transparency leader among various sectors is “Nuclear industry”: transparency coefficient equals 3.7. At the level of international practices and their leaders (I and Premium levels of transparency) are 2 (of I level of transparency) and 3 companies (for Premium) out of 20 companies surveyed in 2017 in the Nuclear industry. A key mechanism that has made the Nuclear industry an absolute leader in the "inter-sectoral competition" for corporate transparency is the industry-wide public reporting system that has been created and developed since 2009.
  2. Among the surveyed sectors of the economy in 2017, not a single one was identified except for the Nuclear Industry, with a transparency coefficient in the high range (from 2.8 to 3.7). That means that there are no sectors of the economy where there would be a significant share of companies disclosing public reporting information in accordance with the international standards. Such a state of transparency in the economic sectors is most likely due to the fact that they do not have systematic sectoral mechanisms (as in the Nuclear industry) that would encourage companies to become more transparent and to disclose reporting information in accordance with the international requirements.
  3. The most common is the average range (11 of 30 sectors of the economy) with the transparency ratio of 1.9 to 2.8. Leaders in the middle range are: "Electrical Energy industry", "Telecommunications and communications" with the transparency ratios of 2.67 and 2.44, respectively.

    Among the eleven surveyed major companies in the sector of the "Precious Metals and Diamonds Industry" there is not a single company that disclosed the information at the level of international practices with verification (I level of transparency). However, two companies provided non-financial reporting prepared using international reporting standards (II level). One company exceeded the requirements of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (III level). Only in accordance with the requirements of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (IV level of transparency) reporting information is disclosed by 4 companies. Due to the lack of disclosure, 3 companies were categorized under V level of transparency. In 2017, one company published only a foreign report, which, according to the study methodology, was not taken into account.

    In 2017, among the 81 largest companies in the "Electrical Energy industry" sector 11 companies turned out to be opaque. 18 companies were categorized under IV level of transparency (they disclosed information on the activities in accordance with the requirements of the Central Bank of Russia). 42 companies prepare reporting in excess of the requirements of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation (III level). Six companies of the industry use international standards (II level of transparency). Four companies, in addition complying with the international standards, verify their non-financial reporting (I level of transparency). This level of transparency in the Electrical Energy industry is probably due to the fact that during the period of RAO UES's existence (before the completion of the structural reform of the energy industry in 2008), the public reporting and the management system of non-financial risks was created and operated in the industry. The observed fact of relatively high and generally growing transparency of the Electrical Energy industry companies is the "echoes" of that project.

  4. Most sectors of the Russian economy continue to be opaque. 19 sectors of the Russian economy have a Transparency Coefficient in the low range from 1.00 to 1.9. The least transparent sectors of the Russian economy are: "Media", "Tobacco Industry", "Light Industry", "Science", "Polygraphic Industry" – these sectors have the Transparency Coefficient of one.

    On average, the number of companies in these sectors of the economy – those who have reached V (zero) level of transparency – is more than 79%, and in certain sectors this percentage is up to 100%.
