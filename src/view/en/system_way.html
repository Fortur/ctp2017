Systemic Approach to Public Reporting

The Practice of Organizing the Public-Reporting Systems

The systemic approach to public reporting is featured by the presence of a number of elements, which provide for the business processes related to the public reporting – such as: the regulatory framework in the public reporting sphere, interaction with stakeholders in the course of report preparation, fixed responsibility for public reporting, etc.

Number of companies disclosing information on the public-reporting system (2016 – 2017)

The need in the systemic approach to the public reporting and information disclosure has been reflected in the new version of the Corporate Management Code of 2014 as recommended by the RF Central Bank14 (Chapter VI: “Disclosure of Company Information, Corporate Information Policy”).

As a whole, the systemic approach to information disclosure and public reporting is not sufficiently developed even in the companies of transparency levels Premium, I and II (48 companies in 2017, and 52 – in 2016).

4 companies – namely, “GNC NIIAR” JSC, IC “ASE” JSC, “TVEL” JSC and “OCBM Afrikantov” JSC (in 2016, there were 2 such companies – the “GNC NIIAR” JSC and “OCBM Afrikantov” JSC) disclosed information on using all elements of the public-reporting system. The regulatory framework is one of the most widely used elements and its presence was recorded by 38 of 48 companies. In 28 of 48 companies, responsibility for public reporting is fixed on a structural unit, in 21 companies – on top management, and only in 6 companies such responsibility is recorded in the CPE of top management. 8 companies developed their own system of indices in excess of those that must be disclosed under the laws of the RF.

Interaction with stakeholders in the course of report preparation is recorded in 35 company reports, while in 10 companies such units as Stakeholder Panels are included in the corporate structures.

In general, we may note the positive shifts in using all elements of the systemic approach to information disclosure by companies of transparency levels from Premium to II in the dynamics of the last 2 years.

The Practice of Interaction with Stakeholders in the Public-Reporting Sphere

Only 37 companies in the leaders’ group of 48 companies (transparency levels Premium, I и II) published in their reports the list of aspects, the disclosed information on which is qualified as substantial. 34 of such companies involved representatives of their stakeholders in the process of defining the substantial aspects. It should be noted that involvement of stakeholders in definition of substantial aspects is an integral part of the GRI Guide and IIR Standard. At the same time 3 companies applying international standards, while drafting their reports, did not interact with their stakeholders in definition of substantial aspects. All of the 37 companies, which disclosed the list of substantial aspects, presented information on how they organized the substantial-aspect definition process.

Number of Companies Disclosing Information on the Actual Interaction with Stakeholders in the Sphere of Reporting

A smaller portion of companies in the leaders’ group (16 of 48 companies) have presented information on their due account of the stakeholders’ requests with regard to reporting. A noteworthy point is that the companies, which do not define the substantial aspects of disclosure (11 of 48 companies) and do take account of their stakeholders’ requests (around 32 of 48 companies), take the risk of failing to present information that would be substantial and meet the interests of their target audiences.
