Transparency of Companies with Different Forms of Incorporations

The form of incorporation is the main driver for companies to disclose information on their activities and management or not. Open joint stock companies (starting May 5, 2014, they are legally obliged to make changes to their organizational legal form of incorporation and to introduce respective amendments to their charters) are legally obliged to disclose information on their activities and management. At the same time, the form of incorporation is a key, though not the only, factor which influences the company managers’ choice to maintain corporate transparency.

ОForms of incorporation of companies of Premium and I levels of transparency

Conclusion:

Companies whose shares are freely traded on the market (public joint-stock companies) are the most numerous among Premium and I level transparency companies. This is due to the fact that for public joint-stock companies (open joint-stock companies, as well as closed joint-stock companies with publicly placed bonds or other securities), Article 92 of the Federal Law "On Joint Stock Companies" provides for the disclosure of information specified by the Federal Law for authorized state bodies and stakeholders. The share of non-public companies, i.e. joint-stock companies, that disclose information about their activities on Premium and I levels, is less, but this is worthy of attention, because the law does not define with the same strictness the requirements for the disclosure of non-financial information of such companies, unlike public joint stock companies (they voluntarily provide to stakeholders exhaustive information about their activities).

Forms of incorporation of companies of II level of transparency

Conclusion:

Among the companies of II level of transparency, companies with the organizational and legal form of a public joint-stock company prevail. Legislative requirements for the issuers to disclose information on their economic activities, etc., have a decisive influence on the company's decision on the extent of transparency.

Forms of incorporation of companies of III level of transparency

Conclusion:

Among the companies that disclosed the reporting information in excess of the requirements of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, there are 34 non-public companies (closed joint-stock companies (CJSC), limited liability companies (LLC), state corporations, joint-stock companies (JSC), (federal) state unitary enterprises). The management of non-public companies begins to see the pragmatics of public reporting and to use it proactively for its management purposes.

Forms of incorporation of companies of IV level of transparency

Conclusion:

46 (23.4% of the total number of companies of IV level of transparency) of non-public companies (LLC, CJSC, (federal) state unitary enterprise, JSC, state corporations), acting on their own initiative, disclose reporting information according to the requirements of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation, , although their organizational and legal form does not oblige them to do so. The reporting of a part of joint stock companies, that issued reports in 2016, was not found on the Internet in 2017 until the end date of the study (14 companies).

Forms of incorporation of companies of V level of transparency

Conclusion:

The prevailing majority of non-transparent or opaque companies (490 out of 564) are non-public companies (LLC, CJSC, federal state unitary enterprise, state unitary enterprise, JSC). However, among non-transparent companies there is an organization that is a public joint stock company (in 2017), the reporting of which was not found on the Internet (until the end date of the study).
