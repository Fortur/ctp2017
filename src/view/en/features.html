Types of Reports Issued by Companies of Premium, I and II Levels of Transparency

"One-volume" Report is a format that involves the implementation of disclosure of financial and non-financial information in one report. In accordance with Federal Law "On Joint-Stock Companies" dated December 26, 1995 No. 208-FZ, the company's management must issue the "first volume" (public annual report of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation) for the shareholders' meeting – by June 30.

"Two-volume" Report is a format that requires preparation of two separate reports – the annual report and the non-financial report (most often – the report on sustainable development). This format continues to be popular with medium-sized companies of the first three levels of transparency, but the inconvenience of such a format for users is that reports are issued, as a rule, within different timelines. Taking into account the fact that in accordance with Federal Law "On Joint-Stock Companies", dated December 26, 1995 No. 208-FZ, the management of the company must issue the "first volume" (public annual report of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation) for the shareholders' meeting – by June 30, "second volume" (non-financial report) is not tied to events and / or dates, and its release is usually made in August-November, when the value of the reporting information is significantly reduced.

Conclusion:

The vast majority of companies of Premium – II transparency levels prefer one-volume reports. At the same time, some of the companies (20 of 48 Premium – II levels of transparency, 42%) prefer two-volume reports.

Types of reports issued by companies of III level of transparency

Conclusion:

Absolute majority of companies that disclosed reporting information in excess of the requirements of the CBR (III level of transparency) use one-volume reports: 144 companies out of 147 (98%). Companies at this level of transparency do not use either the GRI Guidelines or any other international guidelines or reporting standards. Lack of adherence to international standards on the issues of disclosure of reporting information makes it difficult to implement the principle of "comparability" with the reporting of other companies.

Types of reports issued by companies of IV level of transparency

Companies that disclosed reporting information only in accordance with the requirements of the CBR (IV level of transparency), published only "traditional" annual reports. Typically, this is a text document, converted into PDF format. 54.8% of companies disclose reporting information solely on specialized information disclosure sites, that is, it was not found on their official websites.
