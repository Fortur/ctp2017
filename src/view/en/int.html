Compliance with International Standards

Among the companies that follow international non-financial reporting standards (48 companies in 2017 and 52 companies in 2016), such reporting standard as the Guide in the sustainable development sphere by the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) has gained big popularity11. Meanwhile the negative dynamics has been observed upon the comparison with the previous reported year. The GRI SRS12 and G4 both have the basic and enlarged versions. Most of the companies selecting the GRI Guide give preference to the basic version of GRI G4 (in 2017 – 23, and in 2016 – 29 companies). Reports of 14 companies (in 2016 – 15 companies) contain the standard reporting elements from the GRI Guide.

International integrated reporting standard (IIRS), which appeared in 2013, is being applied by 17 companies. As compared to 2016, when the IIRS was only used by 14 companies, we observe the positive dynamics in its application. This standard was intended to support and boost the global evolution of corporate reporting by enabling the organizations to disclose the full set of factors contributing to formation of the value and to certify the fact that such factors are in-built in the corporate strategy.

In the course of preparing their reports, companies also use the AA 1000 series of international standards developed by the Account Ability13 institute. For example, 13 companies used the standard for interaction with stakeholders (AA 1000 SES), and 6 companies chose to apply the standard principes of reporting / sub-reporting по (AA 1000 APS).

117 companies applied at least two international standards – the GRI Guide and the IIR standard – at the same time (in full or in part).
