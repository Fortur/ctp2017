Subsection Disclosure of company activities in the reporting period

The quantity of the companies of the leading group, which disclosed the information about their activities during the reporting period (2016-2017)

The information on the performance of the leading group companies is the most disclosed. All the companies belonging to Premium, I and II levels of transparency disclosed this indicator. Information on the key corporate events that occurred during the reporting period was disclosed by 39 out of 48 leading group companies selected for review. (47 out of 52 companies of the leading group disclosed this figure last year).

Very often the companies used to disclose the main indicators of their activity for the recent 3 years dynamically (42 companies). However only 35 companies provided comments explaining the dynamics, though the requirement of such comments is an integral part of the GRI Guidelines. But it is worth noting that the number of the companies that disclosed the 3 year trends and provided comments on them was even smaller last year.

A half of the leading group companies have disclosed the information on the achievement of the target performance values within the reporting period (i.e.- target and actual values). At the same time even fewer companies have disclosed the targets for the next reporting period (42%). Only 14 out of 48 companies have published the information on the performance indicators for the reporting period and for the future.

The supply chains have mainly been disclosed through a textual description (31 companies out of 48, 64.6%). The reports contain the description of buying raw materials and supplying finished products, show key suppliers, logistics contractors, etc. Only 17 of these companies have added graphic (schematic) images of supply chains to the text. Two companies used only a graphic (schematic) form of presenting this information.
