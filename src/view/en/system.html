Transparency of System-Forming Organizations in the Russian Federation

System-Forming Organizations

The list of system-forming companies contains 197 organizations, including holdings and vertically integrated companies, whose revenues account for over 70 % of the aggregate national income, and the number of employees’ accounts for over 20 % of the total number of workers employed in the Russian economy. The list of system-forming organizations has been approved by the Minutes No. 1 of the session held by the Government Commission for Economic Development and Integration on February 05, 2015 . 10 .

Transparency Ratio of System-Forming Companies as Compared with Other Groups of Companies (by the presented sampling)

Conclusions:

  1. The transparency ratio of system-forming companies, placed in the average diapason, amounts to 2.05 (while the maximal value is equal to 3.6) and does not differ substantially from the average value (2.3). The system-forming companies generate profits accounting for over 70 % of the aggregate national income. The number of citizens being employed by such companies accounts for over 20% of those being employed in the national economy.
  2. 85 system-forming companies (43,15 %) are non-transparent. These companies do not disclose publicly their annual reporting information on their operations. In view of the tremendously important role of such companies for the Russian economy, the share of the non-transparent ones appears huge. If continued, the trend for non-transparency featuring almost a half of system-forming enterprises would reduce the efficiency of monitoring of their operations by the government authorities and other stakeholders. It should be noted that at its first session (of 03.04.2015) the Inter-Departmental Commission for monitoring of the financial and economic condition of the organizations included in the list of system-forming organizations, for the purpose of information and analytical support of its work defined such monitoring vectors as: monitoring of socio-economic development in the economic sectors and monitoring of financial and economic condition of the organizations included in the list of system-forming ones.
  3. 3. The category of system-forming companies includes a small group of leaders in corporate transparency (7 companies, or 3.56% of the total number). None of the system-forming companies was graded as being at the Premium transparency level – that is, none of them has ever prepared its reporting in compliance with international practices, which include verification of such reporting and issue of the interactive report. The 7 system-forming companies disclose information on the base of international standards (transparency level I), while 16 others find themselves at transparency level II. It should be noted that at each transparency level we see companies of different organizational legal forms. The presence of companies with different forms of incorporation indicates that the given form is not a decisive factor for selection (or non-selection) of the vector for the higher transparency. For example, the management of several non-public system-forming organizations, understanding the whole measure of their companies’ influence on the stockholders, regions of presence, and the RF economy, seeks to disclose the reporting information in compliance with the advanced international requirements. Among such companies, we see the “Rosatom” state corporation, “SUEC” joint-stock company, and some others.
