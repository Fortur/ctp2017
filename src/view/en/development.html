Disclosure of information on the activities in the sphere of sustainable development

Disclosure of information on the management system of activities in the field of sustainable development (SD) and corporate social responsibility (CSR)

The number of the leading group companies which have disclosed the information about the sustainable development and corporate social responsibility (2016-2017)

There is a negative dynamics of the disclosure of information on sustainable development (SD) and corporate social responsibility. In fact all the companies of the leading group (44 Premium companies at I and II levels of transparency - 92%, while in 2016 - 49 out of 52, 94%) have disclosed the information on their strategic priorities in the field of sustainable development, but only 30 of them (62.5 % of the leading group) have indicated the key challenges to sustainable development which they are or were facing.

81.25% of the leaders of the group have included the indicators related to SD into the number of the key performance indicators of their companies. 68.8% of the leading group companies have set the strategic objectives / plans related to SD for the next reporting period.

The companies paid the least attention to the assignment of the responsibilities for the sustainable development and the reflection of the progress in this field on the KPIs of management bodies and top management: a total of 19 companies out of 48 (40%).

Disclosure of the information on the interaction with stakeholders for the reporting period

The quantity of the leading group companies which have disclosed the information about the cooperation with the stakeholders within the reporting period (2016-2017)

In 2017 there was a slight decrease in the disclosure of the information on the cooperation with stakeholders. Among the leadership group companies (Premium, I and II levels of transparency) the cooperation with the stakeholders is quite common: 41 out of 48 (85.4%) of the companies provided the list of their stakeholders. This includes 38 companies which describe the main activities carried out together with the stakeholders in the reporting period and 39 companies that provide the description of the methods of communication with the stakeholders.

However, only 52% of the companies mentioned the documents regulating the cooperation with the stakeholders (25 companies out of 48) and fewer than half of the companies (19 out of 48) provided a graphic (schematic) presentation of the relationship of stakeholders with the company and its activities - the stakeholder map.

The involvement of the representatives of stakeholders into the corporate management (either through the creation of the Public board or some other corporate bodies which include representatives of the stakeholders) is less common. Only 13 companies out of 48 confirmed the existence of such a board in their corporate governance system.
